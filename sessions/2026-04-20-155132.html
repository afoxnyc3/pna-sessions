Research: Extracting SKILL.md files from video and audio transcripts

Executive Summary

A production workflow to mine Claude Code SKILL.md files from talks, podcasts, and YouTube content has four stages: (1) ingest with yt-dlp plus a transcription engine (local Whisper for cost, Deepgram Nova for diarized podcasts, OpenAI Whisper API for zero-ops), (2) schema-first LLM extraction with hedge classification and map-reduce over chunks, (3) a narrated-event to general-procedure rewrite pass, and (4) a validation harness modeled on Anthropic's skill-creator evals. The SKILL.md spec is fixed: YAML frontmatter with name and description, imperative-voice body, progressive-disclosure references, all under ~500 lines for auto-loading.

Key Findings

  1. SKILL.md is a two-layer artifact: trigger metadata plus lazy-loaded body.

A skill is a directory containing SKILL.md with required YAML frontmatter (name, description) and an imperative-voice body; supporting scripts/, references/, examples/, and assets/ load only when the skill is invoked. At startup Claude pre-loads only the name and description of every installed skill, and full body content enters context only when a request matches.

Source: Extend Claude with skills (Anthropic docs) -- canonical spec from Anthropic.

Source: Skill Development by anthropics/claude-code -- quoted directory layout and imperative-voice rule.

  1. yt-dlp + Whisper is the community default ingest stack.

Multiple open-source packages (ytpipe, yt-transcribe, youtube-faster-whisper) wrap yt-dlp audio extraction into faster-whisper or whisper.cpp, confirming the pattern: yt-dlp -x --audio-format mp3 <url> then feed to a Whisper variant, optionally parallelized across channel batches.

Source: ytpipe on PyPI -- active package, documents yt-dlp plus faster-whisper pipeline.

Source: yt-transcribe on PyPI -- whisper.cpp variant with parallel workers.

  1. Deepgram Nova wins on diarization and streaming; Whisper wins on cost and local deployment.

Independent 2024-2026 benchmarks place Deepgram Nova-3 at roughly 5.9% WER streaming with built-in speaker diarization and sub-300ms latency, while Whisper is batch-only but deployable locally at near-zero marginal cost. For multi-speaker podcasts and conference Q&A, Deepgram's diarization is the deciding factor; for solo talks, local Whisper is sufficient.

Source: Speko 2026 STT benchmark -- independent ranked benchmark.

Source: Deepgram vs Whisper benchmark whitepaper -- vendor report but with published WER numbers.

  1. Schema-first extraction plus constrained decoding is the reliability pattern.

Production practice converges on JSON Schema or Pydantic-typed output with constrained decoding (Outlines, llguidance, or Anthropic tool-use) rather than regex post-parsing. Field-level trustworthiness scoring (Cleanlab's CONSTRUCT) and canonicalization layers (PromptPort's ROS/CSS metrics) are emerging defenses against "format collapse."

Source: Structured Outputs in Production (Tianpan) -- synthesis of current practice.

Source: CONSTRUCT: Real-Time Trustworthiness Scoring (arXiv) -- per-field uncertainty for extraction.

  1. Unsupervised step extraction from transcripts is a published research area.

LUSE (Berkeley, CVEU 2023) zero-shot-prompts an LLM to segment steps from instructional-video transcripts. Unsupervised Task Graph Generation (arXiv 2302.09173) combines LLM reasoning with clustering to recover step dependencies. Video-Mined Task Graphs (Ashutosh et al., UT Austin/Meta) mines procedural structure across many how-to videos.

Source: LUSE paper -- direct prior art for transcript-to-steps.

Source: Unsupervised Task Graph Generation (arXiv 2302.09173) -- LLM + clustering for step dependency.

  1. Hedge classification is a mature NLP task with shippable models.

CoNLL-2010 established the shared task; jeniakim/hedgehog on Hugging Face is a BERT-based multi-class hedge detector (Epistemic, Investigation, Doxatic, Condition) usable off-the-shelf to strip speculative language before procedure generalization.

Source: HEDGEhog on Hugging Face -- deployable model.

Source: "You should probably read this": Hedge Detection in Text (arXiv 2405.13319) -- SOTA on CoNLL-2010.

  1. Map-reduce summarization is the canonical pattern for long transcripts.

For content exceeding context limits, the map step extracts structured candidates per chunk, the reduce step deduplicates and merges. LLM×MapReduce (arXiv 2410.09342) formalizes this with structured information protocols and in-chunk confidence to resolve conflicts across chunks.

Source: LLM×MapReduce (arXiv 2410.09342) -- formal treatment for long-sequence LLM processing.

Source: Google Cloud: long document summarization -- production patterns.

  1. Anthropic ships a reference validation harness.

The skill-creator skill and mcp-builder/scripts/evaluation.py in anthropics/skills demonstrate Anthropic's approach: run parallel test cases with and without the skill, capture timing and token usage, measure triggering accuracy by testing ~20 rephrasings of the description, and produce an interactive reviewer.

Deep Dive

The full workflow decomposes into six stages that map cleanly onto the requested topics.

Stage 1: Ingest. Use yt-dlp -x --audio-format opus --audio-quality 0 <url> for YouTube and most podcast feeds; opus is cheaper than mp3 and Whisper handles it directly. For RSS podcasts, parse the enclosure URL and skip yt-dlp. Cache audio by content hash so re-runs of extraction do not repay transcription cost [Source 1, 2].

Stage 2: Transcription. Route by content type. Solo talk or single-speaker screencast goes to local faster-whisper large-v3 with VAD filtering. Panel, interview, or podcast goes to Deepgram Nova-3 with diarize=true and punctuate=true; the diarization is what makes subsequent speaker-attributed extraction tractable. Conference recordings with jargon go to Whisper with an initial_prompt containing a glossary. Always persist both the raw transcript and a timestamped segment list so the final SKILL.md can cite [HH:MM:SS] anchors [Source 3, 4].

Stage 3: Preprocessing. Run a hedge classifier over every sentence. Epistemic hedges ("I think", "maybe", "we usually") and Doxatic hedges ("I believe") get flagged, not deleted. The flags feed the next stage: the extractor must either demote a hedged claim to a non-normative "Context" section or drop it. HEDGEhog is the pragmatic choice; it returns token-level labels compatible with segment boundaries [Source 5, 6]. Also run speaker-role inference (host vs guest vs audience) and drop audience Q&A by default.

Stage 4: Schema-first extraction with map-reduce. Define a Pydantic or JSON Schema model:


SkillCandidate {
  name: str (slug),
  description: str (trigger conditions, imperative),
  preconditions: list[str],
  steps: list[Step { index, instruction, rationale?, hedge_level }],
  failure_modes: list[str],
  examples: list[str],
  source_anchors: list[Timestamp]
}

Map step: chunk the transcript to ~3-4k tokens with 200-token overlap, run the schema-constrained extractor per chunk using Anthropic tool-use or OpenAI Structured Outputs. Reduce step: cluster candidates by name and semantically-similar descriptions, merge steps preserving order from timestamps, union preconditions and failure modes. LLM×MapReduce's structured information protocol with confidence scores is the template for resolving conflicts across chunks [Source 7, 8]. Use a CONSTRUCT-style trustworthiness score per field to flag low-confidence extractions for human review [Source 9].

Stage 5: Narrated-event to general-procedure rewrite. This is the most underappreciated stage. A transcript says "so what I did was I ran npm install, and then it failed, so I added --legacy-peer-deps." The SKILL.md must say: "Install dependencies. If resolution fails with peer conflicts, retry with --legacy-peer-deps." The modality shift rules:

  • First-person past ("I ran", "we noticed") becomes imperative infinitive ("Run", "Check for"). Anthropic's own spec requires imperative/infinitive voice, not second person [Source 2].
  • Anecdotal single-case outcomes ("it worked for me") become conditional rules ("If X, then Y").
  • Speaker-specific context ("in our monorepo") becomes explicit preconditions in the frontmatter description or an "Applies when" block.
  • Hedged claims flagged in Stage 3 are either dropped or moved to a "Caveats" reference file.

Prior art on this shift: "From We to Me" (arXiv 2603.03320) treats narrative reframing as abductive reasoning; "Transferring Procedural Knowledge across Commonsense Tasks" (arXiv 2304.13867) studies abstracting stories into transferable procedures. Neither targets SKILL.md but both validate the rewrite-with-explicit-schema approach.

Stage 6: Validation harness. Mirror anthropics/skills/skill-creator:

  1. Lint frontmatter: name is a valid slug, description contains trigger verbs and use-case phrases, body is under ~500 lines.
  2. Triggering accuracy: generate 20 paraphrased user prompts that should invoke the skill and 20 that should not, measure precision/recall of auto-invocation [Source 10].
  3. Task evals: construct 5-10 test prompts that require the skill, run Claude with and without the skill loaded, grade outputs with an LLM-judge rubric, log tokens and latency [Source 11, 12].
  4. Source fidelity: for each claim in SKILL.md, verify the cited timestamp anchor contains supporting content (an LLM-judge check against the transcript segment).
  5. Round-trip: execute one example end-to-end if the skill is executable.

Prior art beyond Anthropic: skills.sh, Tessl, and agentskills/agentskills (16K stars, spec repo) are independent directories and package managers converging on the same SKILL.md shape, which suggests the spec is stable enough to target.

Feynman Bridge

Note: feynman CLI was not installed on this runner; the grounding below synthesizes the cited sources directly rather than a feynman_research transcript.

Think of a conference talk as someone narrating a trip they took once: "I drove to the coast, I almost missed the exit, and the burger at the diner was great." A SKILL.md is a road atlas a stranger can use to make the same trip next week. The workflow is the translator between the two. Transcription turns speech into text. Hedge detection finds the "I think" and "maybe" parts and sets them aside, because atlases do not say "maybe turn left." Schema-first extraction forces the model to fill in a fixed form (name, steps, preconditions, failure modes) instead of writing a free-form essay, so downstream tools can parse it reliably. Map-reduce handles the fact that a two-hour podcast does not fit in one LLM call: you chop it up, extract candidates per chunk, then merge duplicates.

The trickiest move is the modality shift. Stories are in the past tense and personal ("we upgraded and it broke"); procedures are in the imperative and general ("before upgrading, snapshot the database; if the upgrade fails, restore from snapshot"). This is not summarization, it is re-grounding, and the existing research (LUSE, Video-Mined Task Graphs, Transferring Procedural Knowledge) shows LLMs can do it when given a schema and explicit voice instructions. Finally, the validation harness is what separates a plausible SKILL.md from a trustworthy one: it confirms the skill triggers on the right prompts, that its claims trace back to a real timestamp in the source, and that using it actually improves Claude's task performance versus not using it.

Actionable Insights

  1. Pin the transcription router by content type. Route solo talks to local faster-whisper large-v3, multi-speaker to Deepgram Nova-3 with diarization, jargon-heavy to Whisper with a glossary initial_prompt. Cache by audio content hash.
  1. Make the extraction schema non-negotiable. Use Anthropic tool-use or OpenAI Structured Outputs against a Pydantic SkillCandidate model. Include source_anchors: list[Timestamp] on every step so validation can round-trip to the transcript.
  1. Run HEDGEhog before extraction, not after. Token-level hedge tags feed the schema as a hedge_level field, which the rewrite stage uses to decide whether a claim becomes a step, a caveat, or gets dropped.
  1. Implement map-reduce with explicit dedup keys. Map emits candidates per 3-4k-token chunk with 200-token overlap; reduce merges on name slug similarity plus description embedding cosine above a threshold. Preserve step order by earliest timestamp.
  1. Add a dedicated "narrative-to-procedure" rewrite pass. Do not fold it into extraction. Prompt explicitly: convert first-person past to imperative infinitive, convert anecdotes to conditionals, extract speaker-specific context into preconditions.
  1. Copy Anthropic's skill-creator eval harness wholesale. Build 20 positive and 20 negative trigger prompts, 5-10 task prompts with and without the skill, LLM-judge rubric for source fidelity against transcript segments. This is the minimum bar to ship.
  1. Treat sub-500-line SKILL.md as a hard constraint; push everything else to references/. The progressive-disclosure architecture depends on keeping the always-indexed portion small. Move rationale, caveats, and extended examples to references/*.md with their own frontmatter.
